## What Is an Initial Stake Pool Offering (ISPO)

An Initial Stake Pool Offering is a way for a project to rain capital to pay for developers, lawyers and other operational costs involved in building their project.

## Positives of a Cardano ISPO

There are a lot of positives of an ISPO compared to an Initial Coin Offering (ICO) or Initial Decentralised Offering (IDO).

1.  You retain your initial stake of ADA and only give up potential rewards
2.  It is possible to still earn your ADA rewards while earning the new token depending on the project's fee model
3.  No locked contract, you can move your ADA in and out of staking as you please

## Negatives of a Cardano ISPO

1.  Centralisation of staked ADA. This has a direct effect to small pools on the network.

MinSwap went for a different approach to the ISPOs. While many projects created their own pools, MinSwap decided to use existing stake pools in the ecosystem to distribute their tokens. Meld is a DeFi protocol that plans to provide collateralized loans for stable coins in the Cardano ecosystem.

## How to Participate in an ISPO

To participate in an ISPO, you simply need to delegate to that project's stake pool. Search for the projects stake pool and delegate from your wallet. Be sure to check that you have the correct pool as there are usually fake pools that are spun up from time to time by bad operators trying to leverage an existing brands name.

Learn more about [how to delegate to a stake pool](http://cardanode.com.au/delegate-ada-stake-pool-daedalus/).

## Tax Implications of an ISPO

For projects that are taking all of your ADA rewards in exchange for tokens, there are tax implications that you need to be aware of. Every epoch that you are forfeiting your ADA rewards, you are essentially selling your ADA to the project in exchange for their token. As in most jurisdictions, you will need to pay for the tax at the time of receiving the rewards and then again when selling the ADA for the new rewards token.

Please consult a tax accountant or financial advisor to learn more about the tax implications of ISPOs and your situation.

## Diversify Your Staking Portfolio

With so many projects offering ISPOs and other rewards for your staking, it makes it very hard to work out what projects to support and get behind. You can simply pick the project and delegate all of your ADA. In most cases, this might be the best option. If you have a lot of ADA though, you may be able to split your ADA holdings into various smaller wallets and delegate to many of these projects.

A good strategy would be to stake in all of the projects and gain a few rewards from all.

Since the projects also have a limited time in which the ISPO will run, you can move your ADA around from pool to pool to be able to capture rewards from all the different projects.

In between those times, you can also delegate to our stake pool. It will help fund the development of content and provide you with more ways to get the most out of your Cardano journey.
